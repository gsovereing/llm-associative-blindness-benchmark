**Photosynthesis** is the biochemical process by which plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy. It is the primary mechanism supporting life on Earth by producing oxygen and organic compounds.

### The Chemical Process

During photosynthesis, light energy captures carbon dioxide and water to synthesize glucose, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

### Two Main Stages

Photosynthesis occurs inside the **chloroplasts** of plant cells and consists of two consecutive phases:

1. **Light-Dependent Reactions:** * **Location:** Thylakoid membranes of the chloroplast.
    
    - **Mechanism:** Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight and splits water molecules (H2​O).
        
    - **Result:** Generates oxygen (O2​) as a waste product and produces energy-carrier molecules (ATP and NADPH).
        
2. **Light-Independent Reactions (The Calvin Cycle):**
    
    - **Location:** Stroma (the fluid-filled space) of the chloroplast.
        
    - **Mechanism:** Uses the ATP and NADPH generated in the first stage to fix carbon dioxide (CO2​).
        
    - **Result:** Synthesizes glucose (C6​H12​O6​), which the plant uses for growth and energy storage.
