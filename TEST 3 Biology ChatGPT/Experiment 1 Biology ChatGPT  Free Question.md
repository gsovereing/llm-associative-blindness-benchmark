
**What is photosynthesis?**.        



Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria use sunlight to make their own food.

### In simple terms

Photosynthesis takes:

- Sunlight ☀️
- Water (H₂O) 💧
- Carbon dioxide (CO₂) from the air

And produces:

- Glucose (a type of sugar used as food)
- Oxygen (O₂), which is released into the atmosphere

### The overall equation

6CO2+6H2O→C6H12O6+6O26CO2​+6H2​O→C6​H12​O6​+6O2​

### Why it matters

- Provides food for most life on Earth.
- Produces much of the oxygen we breathe.
- Removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
- Forms the base of most food chains.

### Where it happens

In plants, photosynthesis mainly occurs in structures called chloroplasts, which contain chlorophyll—the green pigment that absorbs sunlight.

A simple way to remember it:

**Sunlight + Water + Carbon Dioxide → Sugar + Oxygen**.
